Exclusive Interview

Daniel Goleman on Emotional Intelligence Today

Q: How relevant is EQ in the AI era?

"Paradoxically, as AI handles technical tasks, human skills like empathy and self-awareness become more valuable. Machines can't replicate emotional connection."

Q: Can EQ be measured like IQ?

"We use 360-degree assessments tracking five competencies: self-awareness, self-regulation, motivation, empathy, and social skills. It's more nuanced than numerical scores."

Q: Your advice to young professionals?

"Develop cognitive empathy - understand others' perspectives without absorbing their emotions. It's crucial for leadership in our interconnected world."

Q: How has the digital age impacted emotional intelligence?

"We're seeing a paradoxical effect. While technology connects us globally, it's eroding face-to-face empathy. A 2022 Harvard study showed teenagers' ability to read facial expressions dropped 40% compared to pre-digital era cohorts."

"Digital communication is empathy-light - we lose the neural synchrony that happens in real-life interactions."

Q: Can organizations truly become emotionally intelligent?

"Absolutely. I've worked with Fortune 500 companies implementing EQ programs. Key elements include:

  • Psychological safety training for managers
  • Mindfulness spaces in workplaces
  • 360-degree emotional competence assessments

The results? Google reported 37% higher team effectiveness scores after implementing our EQ framework."

Q: What's the relationship between EQ and leadership during crises?

"Crisis leadership demands emotional triage. During the pandemic, we observed three critical EQ components in effective leaders:

  1. Affective Presence: Projecting calm assurance
  2. Empathic Accuracy: Reading unspoken anxieties
  3. Emotional Containment: Preventing collective panic

Leaders who scored high in these areas maintained 68% higher employee retention during turbulent times."

Q: How can parents cultivate EQ in children?

"The family is the first EQ laboratory. Essential practices include:

Emotion Labeling

"I see you're feeling frustrated" instead of "Stop crying"

Mindful Moments

Daily 5-minute breathing exercises together

Conflict Resolution

Modeling apology and repair processes

Schools using our SEL curriculum report 35% reduction in bullying incidents."
