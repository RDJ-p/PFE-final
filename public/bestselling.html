Biography

Daniel Goleman (1946–Present) is an American psychologist, author, and science journalist who is best known for his groundbreaking work on emotional intelligence. His influential book, *Emotional Intelligence: Why It Can Matter More Than IQ*, published in 1995, brought the concept of emotional intelligence (EQ) into the spotlight and made it a mainstream topic in psychology and self-help circles. Goleman was born in Stockton, California, and showed an early interest in psychology and the workings of the human mind. He pursued his academic studies at **Harvard University**, where he obtained his PhD in psychology. His academic background in psychology, combined with his natural curiosity about human behavior, would later shape his career and thought leadership.

Emotional Intelligence: Why It Can Matter More Than IQ

Author: Daniel Goleman

First Published: 1995

Overview: In this groundbreaking book, Daniel Goleman explores the concept of Emotional Intelligence (EQ) and argues that it plays a crucial role in personal and professional success, often more so than traditional IQ. He identifies five key components of emotional intelligence: self-awareness, self-regulation, motivation, empathy, and social skills, which collectively influence our ability to navigate social complexities, make decisions, and manage our own emotions effectively.
